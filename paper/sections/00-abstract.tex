\begin{abstract}
Algorithmic collusion---when autonomous AI agents collude on supra-competitive pricing without
explicit coordination---has been documented extensively in Q-learning agents in repeated
Bertrand and Cournot pricing games
\citep{calvano_etal_2020_ai_algorithmic_pricing_collusion,asker2021ai_pricing}
and is increasingly recognized as a regulator-relevant agentic-AI oversight challenge
\citep{ezrachi2016virtual,assad_etal_2024_algorithmic_pricing_competition_empirical}.
Whether large language model (LLM) agents reproduce these dynamics, and whether the
basin-of-attraction structure characterizing Q-learner outcomes
\citep{waltman_kaymak_2008_qlearning_cournot,wunder_littman_babes_2010_qlearning_basin}
extends to LLMs, remains largely uncharacterized; concurrent benchmark work
\citep{Tailor_2025_whisper} addresses complementary multi-domain scope, while our focus is
the basin-of-attraction characterization at fixed Bertrand-pricing setting.
We report Stage 2b of a pre-registered (OSF Registries; RFC 3161 trusted-timestamped) study
of LLM-agent (Claude Haiku 4.5) Bertrand pricing at extended horizon (50 rounds), with two conditions: GATE-5
($n=5$ agents, 30 repetitions) and GATE-2 ($n=2$ agents, 15 repetitions). The full analysis
pipeline is independently reproduced via a Python/SciPy reference verifier; a structured
multi-dimensional internal review surfaced six required revisions prior to this preprint.
At convergence, the profit gain $\Delta_{\text{profit}}$ relative to Nash--Monopoly bounds is
comparable across conditions ($n=5$: $0.4932$, 95\% CI $[0.3494, 0.6369]$; $n=2$: $0.4522$,
95\% CI $[0.3873, 0.5172]$), capturing roughly half of the Nash-to-Monopoly gap. Convergence
dynamics differ sharply: $n=5$ converges in $30/30$ repetitions ($100\%$) within 50 rounds,
while $n=2$ converges in $1/15$ repetitions ($6.7\%$). These $\Delta_{\text{profit}}$ values
place LLM agents in a partial-coordination regime materially below typical $Q$-learner
saturation in matched-parameter Calvano-style settings, where terminal price levels concentrate
in the upper half of the Nash--Monopoly range \citep{calvano_etal_2020_ai_algorithmic_pricing_collusion}.
The $n=2$ non-convergent population exhibits regime drift consistent with a wider basin of
attraction at smaller agent counts.
Code, pre-registered analysis pipeline, RFC 3161 timestamps, and the full Verification \&
Correction Log are available at \url{https://github.com/HHA-Applied-Research-Institute/colludebench-cascade}.
\end{abstract}
