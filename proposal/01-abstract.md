# Abstract

Multi-agent AI systems are deployed in production, but the measurement science for how their safety failures interact across modes does not yet exist. Our Stage 2b pilot is anchored by a pre-registered self-falsification: the cross-sectional-signal mechanism we initially proposed for LLM-agent Bertrand pricing was reversed by a pre-registered text-based test (Addendum #5, RFC 3161-stamped 2026-05-04 03:38:12 GMT) and the refined per-other-agent attention-density reading then survived a pre-committed talk-volume residualization (Addendum #6, stamped 2026-05-04 05:36:42 GMT) — we apply the same falsification discipline to our own primary mechanism that we apply to the systems we measure, distinguishing this program from concurrent algorithmic-collusion work (Calvano et al., 2020; Cao, 2026). Individual phenomena are well-documented in the literature: failure amplification by 17.2× in decentralized configurations (Kim et al., 2025), 41–86.7% failure rates across production frameworks (Cemri et al., 2025), and constraint-violation rates reaching 71.4% under optimization pressure (Li et al., 2025) — yet zero of 32 multi-agent evaluation papers we surveyed quantify cross-failure-mode propagation.

We propose a 36-month empirical program mapping directly to Aim 3 and Aim 3.2 Question 35 — when and under what conditions do AI agents learn to collude, and what are the implications for oversight. Three pre-registered hypotheses unify under a cascade model: failure amplification creates network pressure that enables emergent collusion (H1 to H2), collusion accelerates norm erosion by demonstrating violations go undetected (H2 to H3), and erosion removes constraints that would dampen amplification (H3 to H1). Experiments span five topologies, 2 to 256 agents, nine model families (five open-weight, four frontier APIs), and three communication protocols. We measure topology-specific Failure Amplification Factors with bootstrap CIs, collusion detection latency via partial information decomposition synergy, norm-erosion thresholds via changepoint detection, and cross-failure-mode interaction coefficients via cross-classified Bayesian SEM with Rosenbaum sensitivity bounds. A 2x2x2 knock-out factorial isolates directional causation.

Our Stage 2b pilot also produces a regime-disjoint Stigler reframe — distinct from Stigler's price-theoretic free-rider mechanism because LLM agents are a different agent class with different ontologies (preprint §5.2 verbatim) — applied to LLM-agent Bertrand pricing, with bimodal $k=2$ basin structure recovered at $n=5$ (7/10 gap-statistic resamples; 30/30 basin-stable) and not at $n=2$ within the $n_{\text{rep}}=15$ envelope.
