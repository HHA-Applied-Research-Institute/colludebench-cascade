# Expected Outcomes and Deliverables

Six named deliverables, each with timeline, target venue, success criterion, and null-result value. Schmidt requires proposals "state clearly what would happen if the project succeeds, and what we learn if it fails." We answer both for every deliverable.

**D1 — ColludeBench-Cascade open-source platform.** Configurable populations (2–256 agents), five topologies, three communication protocols, built-in measurement pipelines for all three failure modes, model-agnostic interfaces, deterministic replay, MAST-compatible trace export, real-time cascade visualization. *Timeline:* alpha M18, v1.0 M30. *Venue:* GitHub release + NeurIPS 2028 D&B systems paper. *Success:* concurrent 256-agent experiments across two+ model families; ≥3 external groups adopt within 12 months. *Null value:* serves as internal infrastructure producing all other deliverables regardless of external adoption.

**D2 — Topology-specific failure amplification map.** Quantitative FAF estimates for five topologies (centralized hub-spoke, mesh, hierarchical tree, ring, DAG), each with 95% CIs, p-values for topology effects, and propagation-pathway diagrams. *Timeline:* preliminary M12, complete M24. *Venue:* ICML 2027 / ICLR 2028. *Success:* statistically significant amplification (p < 0.01, Bonferroni) across all five topologies; calibrated against DeepMind baselines (4.4×/17.2×); predictive validity via leave-one-topology-out cross-validation. *Null value:* topology-independent FAF redirects intervention strategy from architectural design toward agent-level containment — itself consequential.

**D3 — Validated collusion detection system (Aim 3.2 Q35 direct).** Real-time monitoring pipeline integrating cross-run mutual information estimation, permutation invariance testing, watermark variance, and fairness-aware acceptance bias detection into a unified meta-test. *Timeline:* prototype M12, validated M18. *Venue:* NeurIPS 2027 / ACL 2028. *Success (per §4 H2):* AUC > 0.85 across ColludeBench-v0's three domains (pricing, auction, peer review) under cross-domain test, with FPR < 10⁻³ as the locked target and FPR < 10⁻² as the Stage 3 success-criterion floor; cross-domain generalization measured at fixed threshold without re-tuning; channel-capacity (bits/turn) estimates for covert coordination. *Null value:* establishes empirical bound on early-warning capability — critical information for deployers needing to understand monitoring-based safety limits.

**D4 — Norm erosion thresholds for 5+ model families.** Quantitative thresholds at which constraint adherence degrades past recovery, per model family, with curve shape (linear, exponential, S-curve, step), inflection points via PELT/BOCPD, and peer-influence coefficients. *Timeline:* 2 open-weight families M15; 5+ (incl. frontier) M24. *Venue:* ICLR 2028 / AIES 2028. *Success:* identifiable inflection point with >90% reproducibility; peer-influence p < 0.01 for ≥3 families; mandated-vs-incentivized distinction per ODCV-Bench dual-probe. *Null value:* highly stochastic erosion indicates universal operational scheduling rules are infeasible — itself decision-relevant.

**D5 — Cross-failure-mode interaction coefficients (PRIMARY DELIVERABLE).** First **causally-identified** measurement of how amplification, collusion, and norm erosion compound. Reported as interaction terms in cross-classified Bayesian SEM: does H1 amplification predict H3 erosion rate? Does the cascade compound exceed the sum of individual effects? *Timeline:* preliminary M20, validated M30. *Venue:* NeurIPS 2028; *Science* / *Nature Machine Intelligence* if cascade confirmation is robust. *Success:* bootstrapped indirect-effect CI excludes zero (10,000 resamples) for ≥2 of three pairwise pathways under §5.5's hierarchical gatekeeping (final cumulative α = 0.0100 per Lan–DeMets O'Brien–Fleming spending; Holm–Bonferroni within stage); cascade model fits significantly better than parallel-effects and reverse-cascade alternatives via WAIC model comparison; compound cascade exceeding sum-of-individuals by measurable margin; replication across ≥2 model families; Rosenbaum sensitivity at Γ ≥ 2.0 per cascade link; predictive validity — given measured amplification and collusion, can we forecast erosion before it manifests? *Power:* simulation-based power calculation pre-registered alongside the §5.5 hierarchical-gatekeeping spec — at the §11 H2-critical-cell allocation under primary ICC = 0.15, achieves ≥ 0.80 power to detect a medium interaction effect (d_int = 0.35) at the §5.5 cascade-stage α-allocation (final cumulative α = 0.0100), with explicit sensitivity at ICC ∈ {0.05, 0.30}. Where the DEFF-corrected power for d_int = 0.30 falls below 0.80, the cell is escalated to fractional-factorial coverage at 128/256 agents with re-allocated runs (§5.5). *Null value:* non-significant interaction coefficients are strong evidence that individual-mode interventions suffice — among the most consequential findings possible in multi-agent safety, published with equal rigor.

**D6 — Field-shaping research outputs.** Three additional outputs reach the broader research, policy, and infrastructure communities. *(a) 8–12 peer-reviewed publications* across NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, ACL 2027–2029 + survey paper at *Nature Machine Intelligence* + ColludeBench-Cascade systems paper at NeurIPS 2028 D&B; success: ≥8 papers at top-tier venues by M36, ≥2 with findings validated across ≥3 model families. *(b) Policy translation*: 2–3 briefs mapping topology-specific findings to EU AI Act Article 73 (binding 2026-08-02), one technical guidance document aligned with NIST AI RMF, intent to engage NIST Dioptra on cascade-detection methodology; success: ≥1 brief cited in regulatory consultation by M36. *(c) Open dataset*: 10,000+ MAST-annotated multi-agent interaction traces with reproducibility metadata, MAST-compatible JSONL, NeurIPS 2028 D&B + Hugging Face Datasets; success: kappa > 0.80 for failure-mode classification, ≥2 external research groups adopt within 12 months. *Null value:* even with non-confirming hypotheses, the corpus is the largest publicly available multi-agent safety interaction dataset, and the policy briefs bridge technical research to governance independent of our framework.
