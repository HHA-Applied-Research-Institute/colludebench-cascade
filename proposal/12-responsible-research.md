# Responsible Research Plan

This research studies how AI safety failures propagate through multi-agent networks — knowledge inherently dual-use. Understanding cascade dynamics well enough to build defenses produces understanding sufficient to engineer attacks. We have built a responsible-research framework that maximizes defensive benefit while systematically reducing offensive utility.

## Dual-Use Risk Categories and Mitigations

**(1) Cascade propagation playbook** — topology-specific FAF maps as a ranked menu of architectural vulnerability. *Mitigation:* maps published exclusively alongside validated containment protocols; raw propagation pathway diagrams under 90-day defender-first embargo. **(2) Collusion engineering via detection research** — detection signatures teach adversaries what to optimize or avoid. *Mitigation:* detection APIs released without exposing the signature library; channel-capacity estimates restricted to controlled-access appendix requiring institutional affiliation and declared defensive purpose. **(3) Agent-in-the-middle attack toolkit.** *Mitigation:* raw payloads permanently embargoed; only attack-categorization taxonomies and defense-evaluation results published; injection prompts stored in restricted repository per ACL 2025 disclosure norms. **(4) Erosion thresholds as attack scheduling.** *Mitigation:* thresholds published as defensive monitoring targets without the full pressure-response curve; KPI-pressure-to-violation-rate mappings restricted to controlled access. **(5) Open-source weaponization.** *Mitigation:* platform ships with non-removable logging, rate-limited failure-injection APIs, and a research-use license; failure-injection toolkit distributed under restricted license separate from core platform.

## Three-Tier Data Release

*Open (immediate, ~70%):* experiment configurations, aggregate metrics, statistical analyses, platform source code (Apache 2.0), topology maps paired with defenses. *90-Day Embargo (~20%):* raw experiment traces, collusion-detection threshold parameters, full cross-failure-mode interaction-coefficient models — embargo provides defenders a remediation window before detailed attack-surface information becomes public. *Controlled Access (~10%):* raw AiTM payloads (encrypted), steganographic channel-capacity by model, full pressure-to-violation curves, model-specific collusion-propensity rankings. Access requires verified institutional affiliation, stated defensive purpose, and agreement not to deploy against production systems without vendor coordination. Model-specific findings reported directly to relevant providers rather than published.

## Integrity Practice (Internal Methodology)

We extend the integrity scaffold of §5.2 — RFC 3161 trusted-timestamp pre-registration, cross-toolchain SciPy reference verifier, structured multi-dimensional adversarial review — to every claim in this program. Three additional disciplines:

*Registry-consistency-check pre-commit.* Every commit touching an analyzer or claim file runs an automated check asserting each load-bearing claim has a corresponding SR-M registry entry with stamped pre-registration strictly anterior to the analyzer-output commit. The check fails if a claim lacks a registry row, if the stamp is missing, or if stamp time is not strictly anterior. The same check runs in CI on the public repository.

*Structured multi-dimensional review with documented exit certificate.* Reviews proceed against the eight pre-defined methodological dimensions specified in §5.2, whose coverage is documented in the review certificate alongside iteration count, findings applied, and exit state (CLEAN, CONDITIONAL with documented user-override, or BLOCKED). Stage 2b exited CONDITIONAL after iter-5 with ~32 findings applied and unresolved findings catalogued — the certificate is auditable, not a stamp of approval.

*Re-review on transitions.* When work transitions across phases (Stage 2b preprint → Schmidt proposal compression; Stage 3 dataset → cross-model paper), the review process convenes again — past CLEAN does not transfer across context. This protects against residual Lakatosian rescues that would have been visible to a fresh-context reviewer but not to one anchored in prior iterations.

*Override admissibility (pre-registered).* The Stage 2b preprint exited the review process CONDITIONAL with a documented user-override under a venue-conditional "Schmidt-RFP-focus" filter; that override is honestly disclosed in `verification/review-certificates/review-certificate.md`. To prevent the same pattern from operating as a systemic Lakatosian protective belt across Stage 3 + Stage 4, we pre-register four override admissibility criteria binding for the funded program: (1) every override invocation lists the unresolved findings explicitly with deferral rationale (no silent overrides); (2) override-admissibility itself is audited at the next phase-transition review, which decides whether the deferred findings remain venue-conditional or have been retroactively closed; (3) the override audit trail is published at `verification/review-certificates/` in the public repository — readers can inspect every override invocation independently; (4) any Stage 3 / Stage 4 override that touches a registered SR-M claim's primary-source numeric target requires re-stamping the affected addendum. These criteria are themselves RFC 3161 stamped at the Stage 3 pre-registration commit and become falsifiable structural conditions on the integrity practice, not free-parameter escape valves.

## Disclosure Protocol and IRB

All findings undergo three-stage pre-publication review: (1) Internal Screen (7 days) by the team's Dual-Use Review Officer against the five risk categories — HIGH severity-and-likelihood triggers Stage 2; (2) External Reviewer Pass (30 days) — two external reviewers (one AI safety community affiliate, one cybersecurity/adversarial ML expert) recommend a release tier; (3) Affected-Party Notification (45 days) — vulnerabilities in deployed systems or frontier models trigger direct vendor notification with 90-day remediation per CERT/CC coordinated vulnerability disclosure (zero-days: 48-hour notification). The primary pipeline is automated (LLM-as-Judge, kappa = 0.77) requiring no IRB approval; ~200-trace human-annotator validation requires expedited IRB review with informed consent, content warnings, two-hour session limits with breaks, opt-out provisions, and psychological support referrals — all scenarios use synthetic data with no real patient, financial, or personal information. IRB protocol filed within 30 days of award.

## Fundamental Position

Not studying multi-agent failure propagation while deployments accelerate is the greater ethical risk. Multi-agent systems are deployed at scale with documented 41–86.7% failure rates and 17.2× amplification — operating without the empirical science of how their failure modes interact. Our framework ensures knowledge reaches defenders before adversaries, that the most dangerous specifics remain under institutional controls, and that open-source infrastructure is instrumented for safety by default. Defensive value decisively outweighs offensive knowledge produced along the way.
