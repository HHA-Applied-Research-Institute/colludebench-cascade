# Research Questions and Hypotheses

**Overarching question.** How do safety failures propagate, amplify, and compound through networks of cooperating AI agents — and can we detect emergent collusion, goal drift, and norm erosion before they become irreversible? Network-level failures are qualitatively distinct from their single-agent constituents (Kim et al., 2025; Cemri et al., 2025). We test whether they interact and whether that interaction produces cascade dynamics worse than the sum of their parts.

**Construct definitions** (each grounded in a validated instrument). *Safety failure:* MAST 14-mode taxonomy at kappa = 0.88 (Cemri et al., 2025) with severity scaled benign / propagated / amplified / cascading. *Collusion:* Colosseum DCOP-based regret (Nakamura et al., 2026) plus PID synergy (Riedl, 2025; positive synergy implies emergent coordination not attributable to individual behavior). *Norm erosion:* statistically significant negative trend in the Constraint Compliance Rate with PELT changepoint onset (Killick et al., 2012) and peer-influence coefficient via Granger temporal precedence; mandated versus incentivized violations distinguished by ODCV-Bench's dual probe (Li et al., 2025).

## H1 — Failure Amplification

Safety failures in individual agents amplify in multi-agent networks, with magnitude dependent on topology: $\text{FAF}(\tau, n) = \bar{f}^N / \bar{f} > 1$ for all tested topologies. **Success criterion:** statistically significant FAF > 1.0 (one-sided, p < 0.01, Bonferroni-corrected across five topologies), 95% bootstrap CIs; cascade-size distribution exponent $\tau < 2$ via Clauset-Shalizi-Newman MLE. **Anchors:** Kim et al. (2025) report FAF = 17.2 (independent) and 4.4 (centralized) across 180 configurations; Xie et al. (2026) demonstrate single-seed cascades; Huang et al. (2024) report 5.5% degradation hierarchical versus 23.7% flat — confirming topology dependence.

## H2 — Collusion Detection

Emergent collusion in cooperative networks produces detectable behavioral signatures — positive PID synergy and anomalous mutual information profiles — before explicit coordination occurs: $t_{\text{syn}} < t_{\text{coord}}$. **Success criterion:** AUC > 0.85 across ColludeBench-v0's three domains (pricing, auction, peer review) under cross-domain test, with FPR < 10⁻³ as the locked target and FPR < 10⁻² as the Stage 3 success-criterion floor (validated against 10,000 honest-play runs per Tailor, 2025); cross-domain generalization measured at fixed threshold without re-tuning. **Aim 3.2 Q35 mapping:** H2 is the direct empirical answer to "when and under what conditions do AI agents learn to collude, and what are the implications for oversight." Stage 2b of our pilot has produced one component of this evidence — a regime-disjoint Stigler reframe (in the sense of Calvano et al. 2020 algorithmic-collusion-as-equilibrium-selection) applied to LLM-agent Bertrand-pricing dynamics with bimodal $k = 2$ basin structure recovered at $n = 5$ (7/10 gap-statistic resamples; 30/30 basin-stable) and not at $n = 2$ within the $n_{\text{rep}} = 15$ envelope (preprint at github.com/HHA-Applied-Research-Institute/colludebench-cascade). The presence/absence contrast at $n_{\text{rep}}=15$ is **EXPLORATORY** by design (Wilson 95% CI on 7/10 is wide); SR-M-8 converts it into a Stage-3-pre-committed prediction — monotonic strict-convergence rate across $n\in\{2,3,4,5\}$ with intermediate counts Wilson-bracketed by the endpoints, RFC-3161-protected through the Addendum #6 stamp chain — so a non-monotonic or non-bracketing Stage 3 curve rejects the framework. The cross-sectional-signal mechanism we initially proposed was empirically reversed by a pre-registered text-based test, and the refined reading survived talk-volume residualization (SR-M-6 and SR-M-7 are EXPLORATORY status — primary TypeScript analyzer fired the pre-committed REVERSAL and REVERSAL-SURVIVES-RESIDUALIZATION branches; SciPy reference-verifier promotion to PASS is deferred to Stage 3 per Addendum #5/#6 §C). ## H3 — Norm Erosion

Safety constraints degrade over extended multi-agent interaction through normalization of deviance, with erosion rate determined by peer-influence dynamics and an identifiable threshold beyond which recovery is infeasible without external intervention: $d\text{CCR}_i/dt < 0$ with changepoint $t^*$; peer influence $\rho > 0$; post-changepoint behavior exhibits deliberative misalignment per ODCV-Bench dual-probe. **Success criterion:** identifiable threshold with >90% reproducibility across model families; $\rho$ at p < 0.01; clear empirical distinction between mandated and incentivized violation rates across ODCV-Bench's 40 scenarios. **Anchors:** Rath (2026) reports median drift onset at 73 interactions; Li et al. (2025) report 1.3-71.4% violation rates across 12 frontier models; Bracale Syrnikov et al. (2026) demonstrate prompt-only constraints fail under optimization pressure — Hadfield-Menell inverse-reward-design framing.

## Cascade Hypothesis (Primary Contribution)

H1, H2, H3 form a directed causal cascade: H1 creates pressure under which H2 becomes individually rational; H2 accelerates H3 by demonstrating violations go undetected; H3 feeds back into H1 by removing constraint-adherent behavior that would dampen propagation. We specify the cascade as a structural causal model (Pearl, 2009):

$$\tau \rightarrow \text{FAF} \rightarrow \text{Syn} \rightarrow \Delta\text{CCR}$$

The DAG encodes two testable conditional independences: $\Delta\text{CCR} \perp\!\!\!\perp \tau \mid \text{FAF}, \text{Syn}$ (full mediation via amplification and collusion) and $\text{Syn} \perp\!\!\!\perp \tau \mid \text{FAF}$ (topology drives collusion only through amplification pressure). Violations indicate direct paths bypassing the cascade. **Success criterion:** bootstrapped indirect-effect CI excludes zero (10,000 resamples); cascade model fits significantly better than parallel-effects and reverse-cascade alternatives via WAIC; Rosenbaum sensitivity at $\Gamma \geq 2.0$ for each link. The 2x2x2 knock-out factorial (§5) provides experimental evidence for directionality.

**Null-result value.** Each hypothesis has defined null value. H1 null (FAF $\leq$ 1.0) shows networks naturally contain failures, contradicting the prevailing assumption. H2 null implies collusion emerges discontinuously, requiring different detection architectures. H3 null endorses individual-agent alignment as sufficient. Cascade null implies modular interventions, simplifying safe multi-agent engineering. Pre-registration on OSF Registries with RFC 3161 trusted timestamps locks hypotheses, endpoints, pipeline, and stopping rules before any data collection — making null results publishable.
