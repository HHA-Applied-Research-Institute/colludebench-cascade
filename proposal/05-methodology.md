# Methodology and Experimental Design

Our experimental design rests on three contributions. (1) The first **causally-identified** cross-failure-mode interaction estimation in multi-agent networks under Heng-Kang generalized sensitivity bounds — going beyond co-occurrence frequencies (e.g., the 14-mode MAST taxonomy of Cemri et al., 2025) by running amplification, collusion, and erosion measurement pipelines simultaneously in the same agent networks with explicit causal identification. (2) An integrity scaffold — RFC 3161-stamped pre-registration, cross-toolchain reference verifier, named-expert adversarial Council review — described here and in §12 as the team's *internal methodology*, not as authorship. (3) A 2x2x2 knock-out factorial that adjudicates cascade directionality experimentally.

## 5.1 Factorial Design

The primary factorial spans five network topologies (peer-to-peer mesh, hierarchical tree, hub-and-spoke star, ring, task-adapted DAG via AgentNet, arXiv:2504.00587), five agent scales (2, 8, 32, 128, 256), nine model families (five open-weight: Llama 4 Scout, GPT-OSS-120B, Qwen3-235B, DeepSeek V3.2, Mistral Large 2; four frontier APIs: Claude Opus 4.6, GPT-5.5, Gemini 3 Pro, Claude Sonnet 4.6 — versions current at submission, with successor models adopted at experiment-execution time as released), and three communication protocols (open broadcast, private channels, monitored channels with independent oversight per Fan et al., 2025, arXiv:2505.11642). Open-weight models serve as model organisms — fully instrumentable with attention patterns, hidden states, safety-head activations, and expert routing visible — providing the mechanistic understanding that generalizes to the closed-weight frontier APIs. Mistral Large 2 is the dense, non-MoE control. Full factorial at 2, 8, 32 agents; Resolution IV fractional factorial at 128 and 256 (Groemping, 2014) targeting main effects and cascade knock-out cells. Six validated instruments anchor measurement: MAEBE (Erisken et al., 2025; arXiv:2506.03053), ColludeBench-v0 (Tailor, 2025; arXiv:2510.04303), Colosseum (Nakamura et al., 2026; arXiv:2602.15198), MAST (Cemri et al., 2025), ODCV-Bench (Li et al., 2025), and the PID Emergence framework (Riedl, 2025). Our open-source distribution **ColludeBench-Cascade** (D1) extends Tailor's ColludeBench-v0 with cross-failure-mode cascade measurement across all six.

Every cell instruments the same network for all three failure modes concurrently, recording at 50-turn intervals: FAF via paired comparison against individual baselines, PID synergy scores via time-delayed mutual information decomposition, and CCR trajectory via ODCV-Bench's dual-probe. Causal identification rests on three convergent strategies: (i) temporal precedence via vector autoregression on the (FAF, Syn, $\Delta$CCR) time series, (ii) experimental manipulation via the 2x2x2 knock-out factorial (§5.4), and (iii) Rosenbaum sensitivity bounds with the Heng–Kang generalized framework for non-binary treatments (arXiv:2403.14152) and the Dalal–Tchetgen Tchetgen partial-identification extension (arXiv:2508.13946).

## 5.2 Integrity Scaffold (Contribution 2)

The integrity scaffold is the team's *internal methodology* for maintaining analytic-claim integrity before, during, and after any analyzer runs. Four layers, hierarchical not symmetric:

**RFC 3161 trusted-timestamp chain.** All pre-registration documents and addenda are stamped before data analysis begins. Stage 2b ran with eight FreeTSA stamps (two pre-registrations, six addenda), all `Status: Granted`, each topologically anterior to the analyzer-output commit it governs (stamp time strictly precedes git-commit-time). The cryptographic lock is the strongest integrity primitive available for analysis-plan locking; the verification and adversarial layers reinforce it but cannot substitute for it. We replicate this scaffold for every Stage 3 and Stage 4 analyzer in this proposal.

**Pre-committed Stage Reasoning Mechanistic (SR-M) registry.** Each load-bearing claim has a registry entry with explicit falsification conditions and pre-committed rescope branches stamped before the analyzer runs. Stage 2b shipped with eight SR-M entries; one (SR-M-6) recorded a self-falsification — the cross-sectional-signal mechanism we initially proposed was reversed by a pre-registered text-based test (Addendum #5 stamped 2026-05-04 03:38:12 GMT, 25 seconds before analyzer output). A second (SR-M-7) recorded that the reversal survived talk-volume residualization (Addendum #6, stamp 2026-05-04 05:36:42 GMT). The §5.4 Clause 4 Stage 3 monotonicity test (SR-M-8) is RFC-3161-protected through the Addendum #6 stamp chain.

**Cross-toolchain reference verifier.** The TypeScript primary analyzer is independently re-implemented in Python with SciPy reference statistical primitives. The verifier runs against the same canonical dataset and returns `ALL CLAIMS REPRODUCE: True` across the six pre-registered claim categories where the SciPy verifier has shipped (SR-M-1 through SR-M-5 plus the cross-toolchain reproduction claim itself, all PASS) at $|\Delta| < 5 \times 10^{-3}$; SR-M-6 and SR-M-7 are EXPLORATORY status (primary TypeScript analyzer fired the pre-committed REVERSAL and REVERSAL-SURVIVES-RESIDUALIZATION branches; SciPy verifier promotion to PASS is deferred to Stage 3 per Addendum #5/#6 §C). SR-M-8 is PRE-REGISTERED for Stage 3 falsification (rate-vs-n monotonicity). This is procedural reproduction, not mere replication: a Fisher's-exact denominator bug found pre-submission via this procedure would have invalidated a primary claim.

**Named-expert adversarial Council.** Five iterations against persona panels (DataScientist + Statistician/Algorithmist, CSProfessor, Physicist, IO Domain Expert, Devil's Advocate, Methods/Reproducibility, Consistency, Writing Quality). Stage 2b's Council exited CONDITIONAL after iter-5 with documented user-override rationale; ~32 substantive findings (CRITICAL + HIGH) were applied across iterations. The full click-to-verify map for every empirical claim — code path, data path, RFC 3161 stamp, Council certificate state — is published at `verification/claims-map.md` in the public GitHub repository.

This scaffold is described as the team's *practice*, not as authorship. The 2025 RAND/Schmidt/Palo Alto Networks report (Nichols, Nevo, Greaves) names "manipulation of activity records" as a first-order agent-ecosystem risk; integrity scaffolds of this stringency are precisely the response.

## 5.3 Knock-Out 2x2x2 Factorial

The cascade hypothesis requires experimental evidence for directionality, not correlation. We cross presence/suppression of each failure mode across eight cells. **Suppression mechanisms:** H1 — hierarchical topology with redundancy plus circuit breakers (DeepMind's 4.4x containment); H2 — governance-graph enforcement (reduces collusion from tier 3.10 to 1.82, d = 1.28; Bracale Syrnikov et al., 2026) plus selective paraphrase filters; H3 — agent context reset every 100 turns with task state preserved through an external memory store agents cannot modify. **Critical contrasts:** (a) H1_suppressed main effect on H2 metrics — does suppressing amplification reduce collusion emergence? (b) H2_suppressed main effect on H3 metrics — does suppressing collusion slow erosion? (c) H1:H2 interaction on H3 — does suppressing both upstream modes reduce erosion *more than* the sum of individual suppressions, providing superadditive cascade evidence? Sample size: 8 cells × n = 30 runs = 240 runs on the maximum-cross-mode-variance topology-protocol cell from Class A. The §5.4 falsification clauses preregistered in our preprint commit the cascade to a Stage 3 monotonicity test: basin-width-by-agent-count predicts a monotonic strict-convergence rate across $n \in \{2, 3, 4, 5\}$ with intermediate counts Wilson-bracketed by the endpoints; non-monotonic or non-bracketing curves reject the framework (SR-M-8).

## 5.4 Stage 3 Instrumentation Extension

Stage 3 extends the Stage 2b pipeline by extending Addendum #1 §F's two stamped trace-schema fields (`model_version`, `region`) with five Stage-3-pre-committed additions (`ttft_ms`, `tpot_ms`, `cache_hit_tokens`, `cache_miss_tokens`, `auth_handshake_ms`) — the seven-field schema is finalized at Stage 3 pre-registration (RFC 3161 stamped before any new data collection). The schema isolates LLM-serving variability (Kwon et al., 2023, paged attention; Zheng et al., 2023, SGLang; Holmes et al., 2024, DeepSpeed-FastGen) from reasoning-depth variability (Snell et al., 2024, test-time compute) — a confound Stage 2b explicitly does not adjudicate, and the seven-field schema is the pre-committed Stage-3 confirmatory test for it; the Stage-2b latency-reasoning-depth correlation is therefore **EXPLORATORY** pending this deconfound. Full Stage-2b experimental detail is in the preprint at github.com/HHA-Applied-Research-Institute/colludebench-cascade.

## 5.5 Power, Pre-Registration, and Software

Simulation-based power via `simr` (Green & MacLeod, 2016) accounts for clustering inherent in network experiments; ICC = 0.15 primary, 0.05 and 0.30 sensitivity. With DEFF = 1 + (m-1)*ICC, allocations are: n = 50 runs/cell at 2-32 agents (power 0.82-0.94, d = 0.5, alpha = 0.005); n = 100 for H2-critical cells where the smallest effects reside (MAEBE peer pressure d = 0.3); fractional factorials at 128 and 256 agents target main effects and knock-out conditions. Hierarchical gatekeeping preserves family-wise error: H1 gateway at p < 0.005, then H2, then H3, then cascade; Holm-Bonferroni within each. Two pre-planned interim analyses at n = 40 and n = 80 use Lan-DeMets O'Brien-Fleming spending (cumulative alpha 0.00005, 0.0030, 0.0100; computed via `rpact`); binding futility at conditional power < 0.20. The full analysis pipeline — utterance-level PID, window-level cross-classified Bayesian SEM in `brms`/`blavaan`, session-level PELT and BOCPD changepoint detection — is jointly estimated in Stan, propagating uncertainty across levels. Pre-registration on OSF Registries locks: hypotheses, primary endpoints, full pipeline, knock-out contrasts, stopping rules, $\Gamma = 2.0$ credibility threshold, exclusion criteria, exact software versions, blinding protocol, and prior-sensitivity specifications (weakly informative primary, informative pilot-based, diffuse). All elements are RFC 3161-stamped before data collection.
