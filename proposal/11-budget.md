# Budget and Justification

Total $4,356,500 over 36 months: $3,960,500 (90.9%) direct, $396,000 indirect at exactly 9.998% of direct ($396,000/$3,960,500; never 10.0%), **compliant with Schmidt's ≤10% cap**. Personnel 69.7%, Compute (cash) 1.1%, Subawards 8.0%, Travel 3.4%, API Credits 4.0%, Platform 2.3%, remainder 2.4%. The $4,356,500 envelope reflects the cost required to deliver the eight named outputs in §9 across a 36-month timeline: three principal investigators at 25–30% effort, three postdocs and two senior engineers, two research assistants, a half-time program manager, an 8×H100 dedicated cluster with burst capacity, frontier-API validation, two subaward partners, and the open-source ColludeBench-Cascade platform.

**Personnel ($3,035,000 — 69.7%).** Three principal investigators at 25–30% effort over 36 months: Lead PI Haedar Hadi at ~$47K/yr loaded ($140K total); Co-PI Hass Dhia at ~$47K/yr loaded ($140K); Research Engineer Ahmed Dhia at ~$47K/yr loaded ($140K). The 25–30% effort honors Schmidt's "primary focus for lead investigator(s)" mandate at one full academic quarter per PI per year. The ~$47K/yr loaded rate is below the NSF standard for two-month summer salary alone, indicating modest rather than inflated PI compensation. Three postdoctoral researchers ($1.0M total, $125K/yr loaded — 25th percentile for AI-safety postdocs at Glassdoor's CMU benchmark): Postdoc 1 (Collusion/Game Theory, M1–36) executes ColludeBench + Colosseum + PID emergence + anti-collusion intervention evaluation directly for H2; Postdoc 2 (Norm Erosion/Longitudinal, M1–36) runs ODCV-Bench erosion curves and 5,000-turn longitudinal studies for H3; Postdoc 3 (Detection Methods, M7–30) builds the comparative evaluation framework (paraphrase filters, governance graphs, PeerGuard, Evo-MARL). Two senior ML engineers ($1.1M combined, $200K/yr loaded ~20% below industry median per Levels.fyi) build the ColludeBench-Cascade platform (interception middleware, topology controller, PID pipeline, MAST trace export, 256-agent controller). Two research assistants ($350K) and a 0.5 FTE program manager ($165K) complete the team.

**Compute (cash $50,000 — 1.1%; capacity in-kind via Schmidt) and API Credits ($175,000 — 4.0%).** The 8×H100 80GB cluster + burst capacity for 256-agent peak periods is requested via Schmidt Sciences' "Access to Resources" in-kind channel rather than as cash, matching the work to Schmidt's purpose-built grantee compute infrastructure. Cash $50K covers storage (30–40 TB NVMe + S3 cold archive), data transfer egress, and orchestration/monitoring/CI-CD infrastructure not typically bundled into in-kind GPU allocations. Equivalent commercial-cloud cash budget would be approximately $556K over 36 months (8×H100 reserved at $2.00/GPU/hr + ~30% utilization burst on-demand); requesting in-kind brings the cash envelope to $4.36M, well below the named Tier-2 ceiling. Contingency: post-award CAIS supplementary-access path (no pre-submission outreach made) or cash-reactivation if both Schmidt in-kind and CAIS access are unavailable. API credits cover frontier-model **validation runs** (not factorial cells — the 5 open-weight families carry experimental rigor at zero marginal API cost) across Claude Opus 4.6 ($5/$25 per Mtok), Sonnet 4.6 ($3/$15), GPT-5.5 ($1.75/$14), Gemini 3 Pro ($2/$12), with prompt caching (90% input discount) and Batch API (50% discount) yielding $237–$294 per-run averaged. Stage 2b actuals confirm the envelope.

**Subawards ($350,000 — 8.0%).** Two branches selected via open RFP within the first six months of award; selection criteria published as part of the institute's open methodology. **Branch X — Game Theory ($225K):** one postdoc + 0.5 faculty effort over 36 months at partner-institution loaded rates with overhead-waiver to Schmidt's 10% cap; selection criteria — prior publication on multi-agent strategic interaction (Nash equilibria, mechanism design, auction theory); deliverable test — can the partner's models predict which topologies enable collusion before experiments? **Branch Y — AI Governance and Policy Translation ($125K):** 0.25 FTE policy researcher producing NIST AI Safety standards engagement + EU AI Act Article 73 technical-annex contributions + 2–3 policy briefs; selection criteria — documented record in AI governance / policy translation, demonstrated capacity to engage NIST or EU AI Act technical processes. Selection-after-award matches partner choice to experimental needs as they crystallize from Stage 2b → Stage 3 rather than pre-committing before the science directs the choice; Schmidt's 10% indirect-cost cap is built into selection criteria so final partners are operationally compatible.

**$3M-Tier Fallback Scenario.** If Schmidt offers a $3M-tier rather than $4.36M: drop Postdoc 3, Engineer 2, and RA 2; reduce PM to 0.25 FTE; eliminate burst compute; compressed frontier-API validation (400 runs across 3 frontier families); reduce travel to 2 attendees per venue; release the dataset only without the full ColludeBench-Cascade platform; produce 6–8 papers instead of 8–12; reduce external consultation engagements to ~4/year. **Direct $2,410,000; indirect $240,800; total $2,650,800** (line items in BUDGET.md fallback table); $349,200 under tier. **Honest caveat:** at $3M-tier the program loses 256-agent scale experiments (limited to burst periods only), parallel experimental tracks (reduce 3–4 to 2), the full ColludeBench-Cascade platform (becomes dataset-only), and policy translation (governance partner cut). At $4.36M the program is field-shaping Tier 2; at $3M-tier it becomes excellent Tier 1. The $1.7M differential purchases scale, platform, and policy translation.

H.H.A. Applied Research Institute is a California Public Benefit Nonprofit Corporation with 501(c)(3) status (EIN 41-4991887). Indirect costs of $396,000 (9.09% of total / 9.998% of direct) cover accounting, financial reporting, legal compliance, audit preparation, basic administrative infrastructure, insurance, and organizational management — real operational expenses, not an artificial cap on higher actual costs. Universities typically charge 50–60% indirect rates; H.H.A.'s 10% reflects genuinely sub-10% overhead, directing approximately 1.5× the effective rate of an equivalent university proposal to direct research.
