# Team Qualifications

## Institutional Independence

H.H.A. Applied Research Institute is a California Public Benefit Nonprofit Corporation, granted 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status by the IRS (EIN 41-4991887). This is the PIs' primary research focus; H.H.A. runs no competing program of comparable scope. H.H.A. has no model-provider affiliation, no commercial entanglement, and no conflict of interest with any system under evaluation. Stanford HAI receives Google and Microsoft funding; MIT CSAIL partners with both; Berkeley AI Research operates within Meta's ecosystem. These relationships create structural conflicts when findings — for example, evidence that a funder's model exhibits emergent collusion — carry commercial consequences. For trustworthy AI evaluation, evaluator independence is a prerequisite for construct validity, and H.H.A.'s actual sub-10% overhead means approximately $3.96M of every $4.36M cash dollar reaches direct research (90.9%) — roughly 1.5x the effective rate of an equivalent university proposal under Schmidt's 10% indirect cap.

## Methodological Architecture: Five Reviewer-Replicable Instruments

Every methodological commitment in this proposal operates through five public, time-stamped, reviewer-replicable instruments: (1) **RFC 3161-stamped pre-registration** on OSF Registries for the Stage 2b protocol plus six chronologically-stamped addenda — every analytic decision is locked before data collection in a third-party verifiable timestamp chain; (2) an **independent Python/SciPy reference verifier** that re-derives the analysis pipeline from raw data and confirms numerical agreement to less than 5×10⁻³ — cross-toolchain reproduction makes the analysis falsifiable by any reader with raw data and a Python interpreter; (3) **systematic adversarial redlining** of every load-bearing claim against pre-registered falsification conditions, surfacing six required revisions prior to preprint release; (4) **pre-committed downgrade clauses** tied to data-driven decision rules that fired literally on the Stage 2b data — including retraction of an exploratory k=4 quadrimodal narrative in favor of a k=2 bimodal reading once the bootstrap procedure ran; (5) a **public Verification and Correction Log** preserving every revision so reviewers can audit the evolution of every claim. Every artifact is preserved at the public project repository (github.com/HHA-Applied-Research-Institute/colludebench-cascade).

H.H.A. is a three-person founding team operating an open-methodology research program. The five instruments above are our public review-and-verification practice — open methodology in place of a confidential advisory committee. Upon award, we engage senior external researchers as peer collaborators on specific methodological questions across the three thrust areas — Michael Wooldridge (Oxford; multi-agent systems foundations), Lewis Hammond (Cooperative AI Foundation / Oxford; cooperative-AI failure-mode taxonomy), Christian Schroeder de Witt (Oxford Witt Lab; steganographic collusion), Dylan Hadfield-Menell (MIT EECS; inverse reward design and alignment), and Sarra Alqahtani (Wake Forest; MARL safety). These are project-bounded peer consultations between professionals, scoped to specific technical questions as they arise during execution. Consultation honoraria are budgeted under BUDGET Line I.
